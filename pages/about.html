Iyanuoluwa Adenle

Bio

Iyanuoluwa Adenle (she/her) is a writer and curator living in Lagos, Nigeria. Writing across poetry and essays, her works make a conscious attempt to explore the human condition — grief, loss, and love — driven by a curiosity about how memory, time, and place work, and how we navigate life with language as a witness.

Her practice is nostalgia-driven and research-based. She is currently investigating the in-betweenness of the body, the unreliable nature of memory, and the politics of place — writing into mental health, displacement, sexuality, gender, and religion, and the different ways one shows up in the world.

Her writings have appeared in The Poetry Review, Chestnut Review, Banshee Lit, Agbowo, Lolwe, Tender Photo, Maroko, Pepper Coast Lit, Olongo Africa, and elsewhere. She is the winner of the 2024 Kofi Awoonor Poetry Prize, a 2024 Lantern Art Space Fellow, and was selected for the 2025 Tender Visions Commissioning Project.

As a curator, she investigates work that explores intersectional African identities — creating space for the possibilities of boundary-pushing ideas, and archiving the connections between traditional and contemporary art forms. She has contributed to Nataal Media, Art Network Africa, Pavillon 54, and Omenai, where she serves as Head Writer.
